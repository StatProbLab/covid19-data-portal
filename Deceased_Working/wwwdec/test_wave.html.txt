  <h3> <font color=maroon>Karnataka Deceased Data </font></h3>
 <h4><font color=maroon>Wave 1 versus Wave 2</h4> </font> 
</div>
</div>
  <div class="row">
    <div class="col-sm-12">

<p align="left">We have partitioned the pandemic timeline into three waves.</p>
<ul align="left">
  <li>Wave 1 - till the end of October 2020</li>
  <li>Middle wave - from November 2020 till the end of January 2021</li>
  <li>Wave 2 - from February 2021 till present</li>
</ul>
<p align="left">Since,the counts during the Wave Middle were comparatively low,so,for some of the plots, we only considered Wave 1 and Wave 2 for our analysis.</p>


<br>

<h4>Distribution of number of deceasaed</h4>
<br>
<!-- <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/linePlotAgeDistr.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/linePlotAgeDistr.png" style="width:49%"></a>-->
 <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/linePlotAgeDistrPercent.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/linePlotAgeDistrPercent.png" style="width:49%"></a>
 <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/waveScatter.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/waveScatter.png" style="width:49%"></a>

<br>
<p align="left">The graph on left  represents monthwise death count. Although there has been slight increase in deaths in August 2020, there is a visible spike of death cases in May 2021.</p>
<p> The graph on the right  is a plot of number of deceased in each district in wave 1 versus the number of deceased in wave 2. The point size indicates the sum of deaths during the two waves across districts. The line y=x has been plotted. This suggests that the districts above the line have wave 2 death count higher than wave 1 death count, while districts below the line have wave 1 death count higher than that of wave 2.  <font color=#cc79a7>The number of deceased in Bengaluru Urban during wave 1 is 3836, during middle wave is 488 and during wave 2 is 11235 as of 2nd July, 2021. Due to the large count, this district has not been plotted.</font>
  
<br><hr><br>

<h4>Age and Gender Distribution across waves</h4>
<br>

<p align="left">The first two(from the left) graphs present wave 1 and
wave 2 deceased COVID-19 patients age distribution along with the
census of population of Karnataka for reference. Though the trends in
wave 1 and wave 2 are almost similar, from the graph it can be seen
that the mean age of deceased in wave 1 is around 61 and wave 2 is
around 60.  </p>

<br>  
 <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/wave1AgePop.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/wave1AgePop.png" style="width:30%"></a>
 <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/wave2AgePop.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/wave2AgePop.png" style="width:30%"></a>
 <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/ageAcrossWaves.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/ageAcrossWaves.png" style="width:30%"></a>

<p> In the third(from the left) graph above we plot a stacked histogram of age
distribution across waves.  The deceased population between 0-40 has
increased three fold during wave 2 and around two fold in the other
age groups. In all the below stacked age-gender graphs, the yellow color indicates the males while the blue indicates the females. </p>

 <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/wave1AgeDistr.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/wave1AgeDistr.png" style="width:30%"></a>
 <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/midWaveAgeDistr.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/midWaveAgeDistr.png" style="width:30%"></a>
 <a href="./plotlyfiguresdec/wave2AgeDistr.html" target=top>   <img src="./plotlyfiguresdec/wave2AgeDistr.png" style="width:30%"></a>

<p>In all waves the there are significantly more male deaths then female deaths.  </p>
